Law of Large Numbers & Epsilon

The Law of Large Numbers states that as \( n \) increases, the absolute difference between the observed proportion \( \bar{X}_n \) and the true probability \( p \) will eventually become less than any small number \( \epsilon \) (epsilon).

$$ |\bar{X}_n - p| < \epsilon $$ The shaded red band represents the region \( p \pm \epsilon \). Watch how the blue line eventually stays inside this band.

That is,

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} P(|\bar{X}_n - \mu| < \epsilon) = 1 $$ Essentially: As \( n \) gets huge, the average \( \bar{X}_n \) gets arbitrarily close to the true mean \( \mu \).
